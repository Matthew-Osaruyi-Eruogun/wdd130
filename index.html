Benin City, Edo State, Nigeria

Benin City is the capital of Edo State, Nigeria, and one of the country's most historically significant cities. It was the center of the ancient Benin Empire, known for its advanced bronze casting, rich cultural heritage, and powerful monarchy. Today, Benin City is a vibrant hub for commerce, education, and tourism, with notable landmarks like the Oba’s Palace, the National Museum, and Igun Street, famous for its bronze works. The city is also known for its festivals, such as the Igue Festival, and its role in Nigeria’s oil and rubber industries.
